Perceptual real-time 2D-to-3D conversion using cue fusion

  1. Home
  2. Research
  3. Perceptual real-time 2D-to-3D conversion


Project Description

This system is designed to convert the 2D images to 3D images in realtime. By applying normal distribbution selecting the fusion of prior and cue, this allows converting in constant-time, parallel per-pixel work. They compare their approach to previous 2D-to-3D conversion systems in terms of different metrics, as well as a user study.
It is consisted of two main steps:a pre-process to extract disparity priors and a runtime component. While the pre-process uses many example images and requires considerable time, the runtime components execute in real time.

Authors：

Thomas Leimkühler (MPI Informatik), Petr Kellnhofer（MPI Informatik, Tobias Ritschel(University College London), Karol Myszkowski (MPI Informatik), Hans-Peter Seidel (MPI Informatik)

Impact

Their approach seems to be less sensitive to the variety of scenes and works on priors created by painting. Depending on the problem at hand, working with sensor data can be more or less efficient than their pragmatic approach.
