VECG History

Timeline

1965
The idea was first proposed by Ivan Sutherland in 1965 to make the world ‘Look real, Sound real, Feel real’ and respond to realistically to viewers’ actions.
1968
This was the first VR system that was realized in hardware. Ivan developed the first Head Mounted Display (HMD) device with appropriate head tracking.
1980s
VPL Company manufactured the first commercially available VR devices - DataGlove and Eyephone HMD in 1985 and 1988 respectively
1990
A technology that superimposes the virtual 3D objects on real ones
1992
CAVE (Cave Automatic Virtual Environment) is a VR system which instead of using a HMD, projects images on the walls of room.


The Beginning of Virtual Reality

In 1838, the first stereoscope was invented, using twin mirrors to project a single image and has lead to the first idea of Visual Reality. That eventually developed into the View-Master, patented in 1939 and still produced today.

Important Events

The use of the term "virtual reality" was first used in the mid-1980s when Jaron Lanier, founder of VPL Research, began to develop the gear, including goggles and gloves, needed to experience what he called "virtual reality".
