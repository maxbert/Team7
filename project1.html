String Actuated Curved Folded Surfaces

The ancient Japanese have been devising new ways to fold paper for centuries. In a practice called Origami, the far east has been new paper shapes for generations. Now, as computer graphics technology has become more advanced, we have been able to create more complex shapes than ever. Curved and multi-faceted faces through folds can be procedurally generated by advanced computer imaging techniques. However, these shapes are often difficult to fold and require many different folds simultaneously. To solve this problem, we have engineered a system to use strings to make these folds easier to accomplish.

Authors

  • Martin Kilian (University College London, Vienna University of Technology)
  • Aron Monszpart (University College London)
  • Niloy J. Mitra (University College London)

Impact

With this new technology, shapes that were previously out of reach can now be folded. Applications in fabrication and product design can lead to more complex shapes created with fewer materials. Structurally sound fabrication can now be accomplished in new ways with more effecient folding techniques. Natural motion and movement can be simulated with real materials using the folding process and string method.
